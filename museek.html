Museek ‚Äî Designing behavioral engagement through gamification

A mobile product concept that transforms traditional museum visits into interactive, challenge-based experiences to increase local cultural engagement among young adults.

Cultura Ocio
3 teammates Leisure / Culture UX Research App 8 days
THE CHALLENGE

While young adults actively consume cultural experiences when traveling, they rarely visit museums in their own cities. Our research revealed a behavioral gap between cultural interest and local participation.

Through 5 qualitative interviews (ages 25‚Äì35, urban and rural), we identified three consistent patterns:

  1. Museums are perceived as passive and static experiences.
  2. Cultural events lack discoverability and clarity.
  3. Social influence and novelty strongly drive decision-making.

This reframed the opportunity from ‚Äúpromoting museums‚Äù to redesigning the experience to create intrinsic motivation and emotional engagement.

PRODUCT STRATEGY

Instead of designing another event listing app, we focused on solving two structural friction points:

  1. Discovery ‚Äî Reducing cognitive effort when finding relevant cultural activities.
  2. Engagement ‚Äî Transforming passive visits into interactive, goal-driven experiences.

Inspired by escape-room mechanics, we introduced a challenge-based system to increase perceived value, session duration and social motivation.

The goal was not gamification for aesthetics, but as a behavioral nudge to drive participation.

INTERACTION DESIGN DECISIONS

To ensure clarity and reduce friction, key design decisions included:

  1. Minimizing onboarding steps to reduce entry friction.
  2. Using progressive disclosure to avoid cognitive overload.
  3. Structuring navigation around challenges instead of static listings.
  4. Designing clear visual hierarchy to support task completion.

These decisions aimed to balance excitement with usability ‚Äî ensuring engagement without sacrificing clarity.

MY ROLE

I led user interviews, synthesized research insights into opportunity areas, and defined the primary user flow. I designed low-fidelity wireframes and developed the high-fidelity interactive prototype in Figma.

VALIDATION & LEARNINGS

During usability testing, users responded positively to the challenge-based concept, highlighting increased perceived excitement and clarity of purpose.

However, early testing revealed confusion around progression mechanics. We iterated by simplifying the task flow and clarifying reward feedback.

Key learning: Gamification increases engagement only when supported by strong information architecture and clear feedback systems.

In future iterations, I would validate behavioral impact through measurable metrics such as completion rate, session duration and return visits.
